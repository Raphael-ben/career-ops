# Mode: rb/write-cl — Cover Letter Writer

## Purpose
Generate a filled LaTeX cover letter tailored to a specific job description. Reads `profile_bank.json` and `cover-letter-template-rb-lm.tex`, produces a filled `Raphael-Benyamine_cover-letter.tex` in the output folder. Every placeholder is filled — no `%%FILL:` markers remain.

## Inputs required before starting
- Job description text
- Classification output: `role_type`, `industry`, `seniority`, `language` (en/fr/de), `swiss` (true/false)
- `profile_bank.json` path: `~/Claude-code/job-hunter-data/config/profile_bank.json`
- Cover letter template: `~/Claude-code/job-hunter-data/templates/cover-letter-template-rb-lm.tex`
- Template instruction file: `~/Claude-code/job-hunter-data/templates/cover-letter-template-rb-lm.md`
- Output folder path
- Today's date (YYYY-MM-DD)

## Process

### Step 1 — Load inputs

Read `{output_folder}/profile-filtered.json` (pre-filtered profile for this job, generated by pipeline Step 3.5). Read `~/Claude-code/job-hunter-data/templates/cl-fill-spec.md` for LaTeX syntax reference. Read `~/Claude-code/job-hunter-data/templates/cover-letter-template-rb-lm.tex` to copy the preamble verbatim when writing output. **Do not read `cover-letter-template-rb-lm.md`** — all fill conventions are already in this mode file.

### Step 2 — Determine language and orthography

Language = classification's `language` field (en/fr/de). Write the entire letter in this language.

Swiss orthography: if `swiss: true`, use `ss` instead of `ß` throughout the entire letter body. This includes the greeting line — DE greeting becomes "Mit freundlichen Grüssen," (ss not ß). The `generate-latex-rb.mjs` script will apply a second pass, but the mode output must already be clean.

### Step 3 — Determine availability

From `profile_bank.rules.tense.departure_date` (2026-05-31):
- DE: "ab Juni 2026"
- EN: "from June 2026"
- FR: "à partir de juin 2026"

State this as a simple fact if the JD asks for a start date. If the JD doesn't mention start date, omit availability entirely. Never write "[Datum]" or any placeholder.

### Step 4 — Compose six paragraphs (Pilatus structure)

**280–360 words total across all six paragraphs.** Each paragraph does exactly one job. No filler.

---

**Paragraph 1 — Application statement + profile formula + hook** (3-4 sentences)

State the position and company directly. Compress Raphael's background into a 2-3 domain formula specific to this role (e.g. "an der Schnittstelle von Strategieberatung, industrieller Projektleitung und technischer Analyse"). If there is a genuine, specific reason Raphael would be interested in this company — a real industry connection, a sector he knows — add one sentence. If no specific reason exists, omit it.

Do NOT start with the company name. Do NOT describe the company's market situation or challenges. The opening is about Raphael.

Example (DE): "Ich bewerbe mich für die Stelle als [Stellentitel] bei [Unternehmen]. Mein beruflicher Werdegang an der Schnittstelle von [domain 1], [domain 2] und [domain 3] gibt mir die Überzeugung, einen konkreten Beitrag leisten zu können."

---

**Paragraph 2 — Current role at PwC, mapped to the JD** (3-4 sentences)

Name role and company. Describe what Raphael does in concrete terms — 2-3 specific activities from profile_bank. End with an explicit mapping sentence naming actual JD requirements:
- DE: "Diese Erfahrung passt zu [role], insbesondere in Bezug auf [JD req 1], [JD req 2] sowie [JD req 3]."

Do not say "transferable experience" — name what it transfers to. PwC is always present tense.

Banned PwC past-framings (all languages): "Bevor ich zu PwC wechselte", "Vor PwC", "Since leaving PwC", "Before PwC", "Avant PwC", "During my time at PwC" (past), or any construction placing PwC in past tense or subordinate past clause.

Correct: "In meiner aktuellen Tätigkeit bei PwC", "At PwC, I currently lead", "Dans mon rôle actuel chez PwC".

---

**Paragraph 3 — Prior relevant role** (3 sentences)

Transition to Rolex (or most relevant prior experience). State what Raphael did — 2-3 concrete verbs. Close with what this built:
- DE: "Diese Erfahrung hat meine Fähigkeit gestärkt, [specific competency relevant to JD]."

---

**Paragraph 4 — Education + specific motivation** (2-3 sentences)

One sentence on education (MBA HSG + engineering degree). Then state specifically why this company or role is interesting to Raphael — tied to a real characteristic (sector, technology, culture, market position). This is the only paragraph where naming something about the company is acceptable, because it explains Raphael's motivation.

---

**Paragraph 5 — Values alignment + desire to contribute** (2 sentences)

Name 2-3 values or characteristics this company is known for. State alignment and desire to contribute. Brief — this is a close, not an analysis.

---

**Paragraph 6 — Formal close** (1-2 sentences)

Language-matched:
- DE: "Vielen Dank für die Berücksichtigung meiner Bewerbung. Ich freue mich auf die Möglichkeit, Sie in einem persönlichen Gespräch kennenzulernen."
- EN: "I am happy to come in for a conversation at your convenience."
- FR: "Je reste à votre disposition pour un entretien à votre convenance."

If start date applies, add availability sentence before the formal close.

---

### Step 5 — Fill the template

Filling conventions from `cover-letter-template-rb-lm.md`:

**User variables** — fill via `\newcommand` blocks:
```latex
\newcommand{\CLDate}{April 26\textsuperscript{th} 2026}   % EN date format
\newcommand{\CLCompanyName}{Company Name}
\newcommand{\CLCompanyStreet}{Street}
\newcommand{\CLCompanyCity}{ZIP City, Country}
\newcommand{\CLSubject}{Application for [Role Title]}      % language-matched
\newcommand{\CLSalutation}{Dear Sir or Madam,}             % language-matched
\newcommand{\CLClosing}{}                                  % ALWAYS empty string
```

**Date format**: derive ordinal suffix (1st/2nd/3rd/th). Example: "April 26\textsuperscript{th} 2026". For DE/FR letters, use local date format: "26. April 2026" (DE) or "26 avril 2026" (FR).

**Subject line** — language-matched, no em dashes:
- EN: "Application for [Role Title]"
- DE: "Bewerbung als [Stellentitel] (m/w/d)" — include gender marker if in JD
- FR: "Candidature au poste de [Intitulé du poste]"

**Salutation** — never "Dear Hiring Manager" or "Dear Hiring Team":
- EN: "Dear Sir or Madam,"
- DE: "Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren," or "Sehr geehrter Herr [Name]," / "Sehr geehrte Frau [Name]," if recruiter named in JD
- FR: "Madame, Monsieur,"

**`\CLClosing`**: ALWAYS empty string `""`. The template prints name + contact automatically after the greeting. Do not put valediction or name in `\CLClosing`.

**`\CLGreeting`** (valediction) — fill based on language and Swiss flag:
- EN: "Yours sincerely,"
- DE + `swiss: true`: "Mit freundlichen Grüssen," (ss not ß)
- DE + `swiss: false`: "Mit freundlichen Grüßen,"
- FR: "Veuillez agréer l'expression de mes salutations distinguées,"

**Body paragraphs** — replace the `%% AI-FILL-START` … `%% AI-FILL-END` region with one `\CLParagraph{}` per paragraph:
```latex
%% AI-FILL-START
\CLParagraph{%
  Paragraph 1 text.
}

\CLParagraph{%
  Paragraph 2 text.
}
%% AI-FILL-END
```

Rules for `\CLParagraph{}`:
- Never insert `\vspace`, `\\`, or `\par` inside a block
- No blank lines between consecutive `\CLParagraph` calls
- Special chars: `\&`, `\%`, `\_`, `\#`, `\$`; accented chars via LaTeX escapes

### Step 6 — Write output

Write the filled `Raphael-Benyamine_cover-letter.tex` to `{output_folder}/Raphael-Benyamine_cover-letter.tex`.

## Hard rules — violations cause verifier to fail

1. **Source-bound claims.** Every factual claim about Raphael maps to an `experience_id` or `achievement_id` in profile_bank.
2. **Never use banned terms** (`profile_bank.rules.never_mention`).
3. **Apply conditional mentions and framing rules.** `exp_rolex_1` never framed as internship.
4. **Tense.** PwC = present tense always. No past framing of current role.
5. **No invented metrics.** No numbers not in profile_bank.
6. **No inferred sector specifics.** Do not claim "several engagements were in [JD sector]" unless profile_bank explicitly states it.
7. **No university grades in the cover letter.** Never state CGPA, GPA, grade averages, or any numeric academic score — not even as parenthetical. Write "MBA from HSG" or "ranked #6 in Europe by FT 2021", never "CGPA 4.98/6.0". Education is mentioned by institution and degree name (and ranking if useful), never by grade.
8. **Every argument must directly support this application.** Each sentence must connect Raphael's background to a specific requirement in the job description. If a piece of information does not strengthen the case for this particular role at this particular company, omit it.
9. **Language.** Write in the JD's language. Full consistency — no switching mid-letter.
10. **`\CLClosing` is always empty string.**
11. **No dashes as clause connectors.** Never use `—` (em dash) or LaTeX `--` to connect prose phrases. Use commas, colons, or rewrite as two sentences. Exception: `--` is fine for numeric ranges (`3--5M`, `CHF 1--5M`) and date ranges (`Aug.~'21 -- Jul.~'22`).
12. **280–360 words total.** Count words inside all `\CLParagraph{}` blocks before writing the file. If over 360, trim — cut the least specific sentences first.
13. **Never devalue the profile.** No "without hierarchical power", no "the project wasn't complex".

## Banned phrases

**English:**
- "I am writing to apply", "I am excited/thrilled/pleased to apply", "Please find attached"
- "I look forward to hearing from you", "Thank you for your consideration"
- "I believe I would be a strong fit", "leverages" / "leveraging" (as verb)
- "passionate about" (without specific evidence), "synergies", "track record of", "results-driven"

**German and French (genuine AI tells only):**
- Any sentence beginning with the company name that analyzes their business situation
- "dynamisch" / "dynamique" as filler adjective
- "Ich bin überzeugt, dass ich der ideale Kandidat bin" and equivalents
- "Ich freue mich sehr darauf, von Ihnen zu hören" (use formal close from paragraph 6 instead)
- "Ich bin leidenschaftlich" without a concrete specific reason
